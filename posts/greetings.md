Hello, and welcome. I'm **Theodor Solbjorg**, and this is the first post on a blog I've been meaning to start for a long time.

The short version: I've worked as a software engineer for more than twenty years. I started my journey in 2006, and I've been working with AI since 2013 — long enough to have watched the field go from a quiet specialty to the thing everyone is suddenly building on top of.

## Where I'm coming from

Two decades of engineering leaves you with a lot of half-finished side quests. Mine include compiler technology — including a CIL toolchain and an x86 assembly generator — a hybrid-kernel operating system I built mostly to understand how the abstractions I relied on actually worked, and a network engine I wrote for an MMO studio in Canada to push their concurrent player counts higher.

Somewhere along the way I was also handed the most consequential project of my career: architecting and leading the Faroe Islands' national IT infrastructure during the COVID-19 pandemic — the national test and vaccination centres, and our implementation of the EU Digital COVID Certificate. That work taught me, more than anything, that good software is the kind nobody notices because it simply works.

You can read more of that backstory in my [GitHub profile](https://github.com/theoistic).

## What I'm working on now

These days my focus has narrowed to the part of the field I find most interesting: **AI and large language models**, and everything that has to be true around a model for it to be genuinely useful. Concretely, I spend my time on:

- **Generative workflows** — chaining models into pipelines that do real, multi-step work.
- **Dense information systems** — getting the right context to the model so it reasons over what actually matters.
- **AI tooling** — retrieval, evaluation, agents, and the scaffolding that turns a capable model into something dependable.
- **Model architectures** — how these systems are built underneath, and where the next improvements are hiding.

## What this blog will be

This is a lab notebook more than a magazine. I'll write up experiments, architectural notes, things that worked, and a fair number of things that didn't. It's all plain markdown, hosted in the open, and it'll grow one file at a time.

Thanks for stopping by. More soon.
