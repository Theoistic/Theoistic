I'm Theodor Solbjorg — a software engineer with more than twenty years behind the keyboard. I started my software journey in 2006, began working seriously with AI in 2013, and I've spent the years since chasing the same question from different angles: how do we build systems that are both genuinely capable and genuinely understandable?

## What I work on

These days my attention is on AI and large language models — specifically the parts that decide whether a model is actually useful in production:

- **Generative workflows** — orchestrating models into pipelines that do real work, not just one-shot prompts.
- **Dense information systems** — packing the right context into the right place so models reason over what matters.
- **AI tooling** — the unglamorous scaffolding (retrieval, evaluation, agents) that turns a model into a product.
- **Model architectures** — how these systems are built underneath, and where the next gains come from.

## The long way round

Before AI took over my time I worked across a wide stack — C# .NET, Python, C/C++, Assembly, JavaScript and plenty more — and on a few things I'm still proud of: compiler technology (CIL and an x86 assembly generator), a hybrid-kernel operating system, and a network engine built for an MMO studio in Canada to scale their concurrent player base. I'm from the Faroe Islands, speak a handful of languages natively, and am slowly adding Japanese and French to the list.

> I'd rather ship something clear and occasionally wrong than something clever and unfalsifiable.

## A note on the work that mattered most

I served as architect and lead engineer for the Faroe Islands' national IT infrastructure during the COVID-19 pandemic — standing up the national test and vaccination centres and the Faroese implementation of the EU Digital COVID Certificate (KoronaPass). It was a reminder that software is at its best when it quietly carries something important.

This is the lab notebook. I write things down as I figure them out — the working ideas and the dead ends both. Everything here is plain markdown, hosted in the open. If any of it resonates, reach out.
